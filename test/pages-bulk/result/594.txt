                      <h>Art & Special Effects Tutorials

     
   <h>A+ Tutorials 
<p>-- Tutorials by Janee. Learn basic retouching, simple
   shapes, lettering effects, burned edges, linking letters, how to make
   a snowflake, the best tutorial on using the pen tool, wood and more.

   <h>Absolute Cross   
<p>PS tutorials on text effects, interfaces, special
   effects, textures and basics. Also some good webmaster tutorials.

   <h>Adobe's Photoshop Tutorials 
<p>Tutorials from the source. Many fun
   projects here. Be sure to check in from time to time to try out the
   new ones.

   <h>Alan Hettinger  
<p>Nice specular highlighted button and a tut for cool
   graffiti... and more!

   <h>Al's Photoshop Tutorial  
<p>Al takes us step by step through making a
   sketch into a nicely colored and inked cartoon drawing with Photoshop.

   <h>AQ Art & Design  
<p>Anders Qvicker has a nice set of recipes for special
   effects. I like his rusted lattice and stone letters best! Hit the
   Home button to see his cool Flash interface!

   <h>ArtWorld 
<p>Many cool effects and techniques here. The best ones are tuts
   in which the artist describes how a piece of art was done.

   <h>Asylum1  
<p>Several fun artistic effects here, with precise instructions.

   <h>Basic Photoshop  
<p>You have to sign up to get to these tutorials on
   basic PS tools and their applications.

   <h>Bicerebral  
<p>Many fine recipes for some popular effects including text
   effects, pipes, glass buttons, and patterns.

   <h>Big Big Truck Tutorial  
<p>Follow along  through the creation of a
   cartoon character from the sketch through coloring.

   <h>Computer Arts Tutorials  
<p>Many tutorials for learning the basics of
   Photoshop. Includes compositing images, using some of the version 6
   tools, filters and effects, fixing bad lighting, and more. Includes
   some tuts for other apps too.

   <h>Digitalhints  
<p>Several very basic tutorials, some with viewlets,
   offering such effects as making wires, fire text, and replacing a
   color.

   <h>The Continuum  
<p>A few good basic tutorials including a good chrome and
   a nice basic one on using a mask for a "fade out" effect.

   <h>DaVinci Graphics  
<p>Down to earth tutorials for creation of almost
   by-hand effects including chrome-effect gold text, lightening, and a
   3D text. best discussion on "how to be an artist".

   <h>DesignsByMark  
<p>Many resources for Photoshoppers tucked in between his
   advertising.

   <h>Digitalhints  
<p>Several straightforward tutorials on topics like screws,
   simple buttons, and wires. You can also now get pdf downloads of some
   of the tuts or watch viewlets.

   <h>Elated  
<p>This link takes you past all their annoying entrance screens
   straight to the tuts. The best ones are lighting effects, a few
   gradient tips, how to make a rollover.

   <h>e-Scrappers 
<p>Scrapbooking is a fun application of Photoshop or
   Elements. You can learn more about this fun hobby here... and have a
   look at the fine effects tutorials too!

   <h>Eyeball Design  
<p>Some nice tutorials for "high tech" effects in a very
   attractive site.

   <h>Eye Digital Emotion  
<p>How-to's for some nice effects, separated by
   levels. I like Alien Orbs, the Metallic Text effect, and the Wall
   Texture.

   <h>80Four  
<p>A few special effects tutorials at the beginner level. I like
   the shattered text one.

   <h>Extreme Photoshop  
<p>Several cool special effects tuts. Scanlines,
   tentacles, and a soccer ball are among the topics.

   <h>GFX Artist  
<p>Very nice collection of tutorials which deal with digital
   painting, from sketch to completion.

   <h>Graphx Design Tutorials  
<p>Once you make it through the ads to the
   tutorials, you will find some really good ones here. I like the "runny
   text" that looks a bit like blood, the metal plate with screws, and
   the flag in the wind.

   <h>Guru's  
<p>Click Home in the "Go back to" to get to all the tuts. I just
   take you to this one because it is the best one on the use of the
   displacement filter, (till i write mine).

   <h>Hands On  
<p>A few very fine tutorials on topics like how to make a glass
   button, masks, lighting effects, and more.

   <h>Heathrowe 
<p>Some nice tutorials including some patterns, and 3D letter
   text.

   <h>Janee's Photoshop Tutorials  
<p>A fine set of tutorials by Janee. Learn
   basic retouching, simple shapes, lettering effects, use of the pen
   tool, burned edges, linking letters, how to make a snowflake, the best
   tutorial on use of the pen tool, wood and more.

   <h>Jason's Graphics Tutorials  
<p>Annoying popups, but some very nice
   tutorials, including an interesting approach to chrome These are for
   PS 5, but will work for other versions.

   <h>Jeff's Photoshop Tips & Tricks  
<p>Nice little list of short beginners'
   tutorials on such things as bevelled buttons, tiled backgrounds, and
   TV lines.

   <h>JP's PSP Tutorials 
<p>Yes.. I know that PSP is Paint Shop Pro and not
   Photoshop, but these ideas work nicely with Photoshop, too. I really
   like the opal text!

   <h>Laurie McCanna's Photoshop Tips   
<p>She calls 'em "tips" but what they
   are is actually short tutorials on topics such as how to make a bevel
   without filters or layer effects, layers, and custom brushes. LOTS of
   great stuff here for all of us.

   <h>Lombergar.com  
<p>Several fine tutorials done in Quicktime movie format.
   I like the microscopic effect and the grunge type.

   <h>Magic's Photoshop Tutorials  
<p>From beginner on up, lots of good
   tutorials and a very neat interface! Learn how to make buttons,
   interfaces, slicing, compositing, and other basics. Uses version 4 but
   much of the stuff still is good.

   <h>NASA Huh? 
<p>Well, this link takes you to instructions for how to make 3D
   pictures, visible with 3D glasses!

   <h>Nebulus Designs Photoshop in 21 Days  
<p>He doesn't have all 21 days, but
   what he does have is a fantastic resource and a neat and tidy course
   in beginning Photoshop. Learn about "gamuts", color palettes, lighting
   effects, much more!

   <h>Pegasus Web Design Resources  
<p>Nice collection of tutorials for making
   website headers and interface designs. Other topics include textures
   and some special effects.

   <h>Perfect Pixels Photoshop Tutorials  
<p>Several nice effects here.
   Includes some text effects, textured metal, water, rough stone, drippy
   text, and a page curl. Version 5, but most things apply across
   versions.

   <h>Phong 
<p>Fun effects in a really trippy site with kicky navigation.. but
   don't be in a hurry. I particularly like the wires and the e-plastic
   text.

   <h>Photoshop Cafe  
<p>Several nicely done tutorials for effects such as
   chrome, water droplets, getting rid of a background (extract tool) and
   brushed metal. Also one on rollovers in ImageReady.

   <h>Photoshop Design.NET 
<p>You have to jump through a hoop to get to the
   link for the tutorials, but it's worth it for many tutorials on
   creating photomontages.

   <h>Photoshop Guru's Handbook  
<p>All kinds of cool PS things, including a
   great list of tutorials for newbies through advanced. Includes text
   effects, scanlines, plastic effects, much more. With this author's
   methods of teaching, you can learn to solve your problems rather than
   just following a recipe.

   <h>Photoshop Techniques  
<p>Fire and rain! Just the 2 tuts so far, but this
   is the best tut on making of realistic fire that i've seen! (Once you
   click in, choose Techniques from the top interface.)

   <h>Production Graphics w/ Wendy Peck  
<p>Many tutorials for PS and for other
   graphics programs too including Illustrator, PSP, Corel Draw and
   Fireworks. Also see the Graphics Greats section where graphics
   professionals are interviewed, and don't miss Dan Giordan's PS
   tutorials.

   <h>PS6.com 
<p>Richard Lynch, author of Using Photoshop 6, presents several
   really nicely done tutorials. Topics include basic color correction,
   creating transparency for the web, symmetrical shapes, and the extract
   tool. He is moving more tutorials to his site, as well.

   <h>Raytracing in Photoshop  
<p>Learn how to make some very realistic 3D
   effects using this technique. This is NOT a beginners' tutorial!

   <h>scotty.cc 
<p>Just a few tutorials, but they are good ones for fun
   effects. I like the color changing cars.

   <h>Shades' Space Tutorial  
<p>Follow Shades through the creation of a neat
   space cloud with stars. He has just the one tutorial right now, but
   you should find this worth your trip!

   <h>Shanzcan  
<p>Shan has it going on! Many excellent tutorials including
   some geometric topics, photo enhancement, and general study materials
   for PS.

   <h>Sitepoint  
<p>One tutorial which is an excellent starting point for
   making "grunge" collages.

   <h>Spoono  
<p>Some neat tuts for effects. Includes brushes, chrome, grunge
   backgrounds, lighting effects, neon, and adding color to part of an
   image.

   <h>Stewart Dean's Webspace  
<p>Some very nice tutorials including the best
   for chrome on the 'net.

   <h>Stickysauce  
<p>Among the PS tuts, there are some standard text effects,
   retouching, tv scanlines, and more. These are strictly recipe-type
   directions.

   <h>Striding Studios  
<p>Some nice special effects, text effects, cool grunge
   dust and wireframes.

   <h>Sue Chastain's Original Tutorials  
<p>Several fun how-tos including
   making a snow globe, a seamless pattern with cow spots, and see-thru
   text from the PS Guide for About.com. You will have to deal with a
   couple of About's annoying pop-ups.

   <h>Team Photoshop  
<p>Many really good tutorials for some neat effects. best
   gel text and they approach some beginner topics in a humourous way.

   <h>ThinkDan Tutorials  
<p>A fine set of tutorials for several graphics
   programs including a large group of Photoshop tutorials. His interface
   wasn't working when i visited last, but this listing will take you to
   topics from goop text and photo retouching to making a CD-like image.

   <h>Tioem 
<p>It's T-O-M and his tutorials! Though there are only 6 so far, I
   like them. I particularly like the stencil-like images made from a
   photo and the tribal art.

   <h>Trevor Morris  
<p>Many really great tutorials from Trevor including the
   best one for rounded corners, a neat ray of light effect, and many
   more.

   <h>t-Tutorials.com 
<p>Many cool effects including the best lightning
   tutorial that I've found!

   <h>Tutorial Tomb 
<p>Site includes many tutorials for varied effects,
   signature artwork, and more. Site also includes an active forum.

   <h>Van Der Ree's Page  
<p>best tutorial on putting text into a circle or an
   oval using polar coordinates.

   <h>VDL - Tutorials - Graphics  
<p>Exact cropping for feathered-edge
   graphics, backlighting, and a cool "design sketch lines" tutorial.
   Different stuff, well-said.

   <h>Vecpix  
<p>Many effects-based tutorials for Photoshop. Most are pretty
   stock, but I like several including the Luminous Essence and
   LCD Screen. Water drops is the best of this effect!

   <h>Wacom Tip Archive  
<p>Making a photo into a painting, hand-drawn
   textures, and many more effects in these short tutorials with emphasis
   on use of the Wacom tablet.

   <h>WebClass.RU  
<p>Everything i could find is in English. And... what a nice
   collection of tutorials and other resources! Effects include pearls,
   gridwork, some neat backgrounds,

   <h>Web Developer: Site Design & Graphics  
<p>A few nice beginner's tutorials
   like making an interface and several text effects. Topics include
   Photoshop, general graphic design principles, and website design. Has
   several neat PS effects like burning text, chalk and dripping text.

   <h>WebGFX Center  
<p>A fine group of interesting beginner-level tutorials
   including chrome, color blending, cutout, graffiti, metallic textures,
