<h> - A -

<l>   archaeologist
<p>          a scientist who studies the remains of past and present humans

<l>   artifact
<p>          an object made, or modified by humans

<l>   atl-atl
<p>          a bone or wood shaft implement, held in one hand, and used to
          propel a spear.  The tool functions as a lever, giving greater
          thrust and distance to the spear or dart being thrown

<l>   awl
<p>          a small pointed hand tool used for piercing holes in leather,
          wood, and other materials

     _________________________________________________________________

<h> - B -

<l>   back dirt
<p>          the excavated material from a site, presumed to be of little or
          no further archaeological significance

<l>   banner stone
<p>          a stone that was attached to an atl-atl in order to make it a
          more effective weapon by adding weight and balance

<l>   behavior
<p>          the way a person acts

<l>   brochure
<p>          promotional literature

<l>   bundle burial
<p>          human bones bundled together in some material and buried

<l>   burial mound
<p>          mound under which a person or group of people were buried

     _________________________________________________________________

<h> - C -

<l>   cardinal directions
<p>          north, south, east, west

<l>   catlinite
<p>          another name for pipestone, a reddish sandstone used by Native
          Americans for making pipes

<l>   celt
<p>          a small axe-like type of stone implement usually held in the
          hand used for working wooden materials

<l>   cemetery
<p>          a location where individuals are buried

<l>   ceremony
<p>          a gathering of people for a program, usually serious in nature,
          for a specific purpose

<l>   chert
<p>          a fine grained sedimentary rock that is white, pinkish, brown,
          gray or blue-gray in color.  It is often shaped into stone
          artifacts by chipping

<l>   clay
<p>          material used for making pots

<l>   clovis point
<p>          large stone projectile point used by early American hunters to
          kill large game animals

<l>   compass rose
<p>          used to show direction on a map

<l>   compound mound
<p>          mounds that are made up of conical mounds connected by linear
          mounds

<l>   conical mound
<p>          a cone or oval shaped mound that usually contains human burials

<l>   copper breast ornament
<p>          a piece of pounded natural copper

<l>   coulees
<p>          steep sided valleys found along the Upper Mississippi Waterway

<l>   cremation
<p>          burials that are made from the remains of burned human bones

<l>   cremation ashes
<p>          fragments of charred human bones

<l>   cultural diffusion
<p>          the spreading of cultural traits from one society to another
          through trade or other forms of contact

<l>   culture
<p>          way of life

<l>   custom
<p>          established practice; habit; tradition

<h> - D -

<l>   driftless area
<p>          parts of NE Iowa, SE Minnesota, and SW Wisconsin that weren't
          affected by the most recent ice age

     _________________________________________________________________

<h> - E -

<l>   effigy mound
<p>          mounds that are build in the shape of animals or birds

<l>   erosion
<p>          soil that is eaten away by water or wind

<l>   estimation
<p>          probable calculation or valuation

<l>   excavated
<p>          hollowed out cavity; explored

<l>   extended burial
<p>          the body is laid out flat, usually on the back

     _________________________________________________________________

<h> - F -

<l>   fantasy
<p>          stories set in an unreal world.  Fantasy is based upon the
          imagination in one's mind

<l>   farming
<p>          act of growing crops on the land

<l>   feature
<p>          evidences of human activity at a site visible as disturbances
          in the soil.  Some examples would be post molds, storage pits,
          or hearths

<l>   flexed burial
<p>          a body that is buried in the fetal position

<l>   folsom point
<p>          large stone projectile point used by early American hunters to
          kill large game animals

     _________________________________________________________________

<h> - G -

<l>   grave goods
<p>          objects placed with human burials meant to equip the person for
          the after life

<l>   grit
<p>          crushed stone or sand placed in clay in order to make potter
          vessels stronger

     _________________________________________________________________

<h> - H -

<l>   haiku
<p>          brief poem of seventeen syllables

<l>   hide scraper
<p>          a tool made out of bone or stone used for removing the flesh
          from an animal hide

<l>   Hopewellian/Hopewell
<p>          refers to a prehistoric culture from Ohio known for elaborate
          burial practices

<l>   horticulture
<p>          plant cultivation carried out with relatively simple tools and
          methods

<l>   humerus
<p>          the large upper arm bone

     _________________________________________________________________

<h> - I -

<l>   intermediate directions
<p>          northeast, northwest, southeast, southwest

<l>   interview
<p>          a record of a conversation between an interviewer and a
          subject.  The intent of the interviewer is to provide for
          readers an overview and understanding of the subject being
          interviewed.

<h> - L -

<l>   legend
<p>          a story handed down from earlier times

<l>   linear mound
<p>          mound built in the shape of a cigar or candy bar

<l>   littering
<p>          unwanted debris

<l>   living

<h> - M -

<l>   man-made changes
<p>          any change caused by humans

<l>   mano and metate
<p>          stone implements used for grinding nuts, seeds, and other
          foodstuffs

<l>   map key
<p>          used to show features on a map

<l>   map scale
<p>          used to show distance on a map

<l>   medicinal
<p>          items use for a medical purpose, usually to cure or relieve a
          disease

<l>   mega fauna
<p>          the name given to the large ice age animals (i.e. mammoths and
          mastodons) that inhabited North America at the time of arrival
          of the Early Americans

<l>   Mississippi River
<p>          river to the east of Iowa

<l>   myth
<p>          a tradition or fable; an invented story

<h> - N -

<l>   National Park Proclamation
<p>          special distinction given to an area for the preservation and
          public education of a historic site

<l>   natural changes
<p>          any change as a result of weather or other normal occurrence

<l>   natural resource
<p>          a material that comes from the earth

<l>   nature
<p>          environment

<l>   negative
<p>          an effect that is not in the best interest of something

<l>   non-living
<p>          matter or material that is not alive

<l>   non-renewable resource
<p>          something that can not be replaced

     _________________________________________________________________

<h> - O -

<l>   observer
<p>          one who observes; a spectator who takes notice of something

<l>   obsidian
<p>          a type of black stone (volcanic glass) found in the Western
          U.S.

<l>   open pit mine
<p>          a large scale mining operation that removes materials from the
          surface of a site.   A large hole or "pit" remains unless the
          land is reshaped

<l>   outline
<p>          the line surrounding an object

     _________________________________________________________________

<h> - P -

<l>   phalanges
<p>          human finger bones

<l>   pipestone
<p>          a soft red sandstone from a quarry in Southwestern Minnesota
          used for making pipes

<l>   pollution
<p>          contamination of soil, water, or air

<l>   positive
<p>          an effect that is certain to be good for something

<l>   pot hunter
<p>          person who digs or picks up archaeological materials without
          permission in order to recover goods

<l>   pot sherd (shard)
<p>          an individual piece of a broken pottery vessel

<l>   projectile point
<p>          the tip of an arrow or atl atl dart, often made out of stone

<h> - T -

<l>   tempering agent
<p>          sand, stone, shell, clay or plant fibers added to local clays
          to prevent pottery from shrinkage and cracking during firing or
          drying

<l>   totem
<p>          a plant or animal associated with a clan as a means of group
          identification.  It may have other significance for the group
          as well

<l>   transcript
<p>          a record of a conversation.  Transcripts may be obtained from
          interviews, trials, or someone's recollections
